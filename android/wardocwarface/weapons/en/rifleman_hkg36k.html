Historical note

Heckler & Koch Gewehr 36 (G36 K) is a family of firearms developed in the early 1990s by the German company Heckler & Koch & to replace the well-known HK G3 automatic rifle. HK G3 (it. Gewehr 3 — Rifle 3) is a German automatic rifle adopted by the German army in 1959. In 1995 it was replaced by the automatic caliber 5.56 mm NATO HK G36.

From the structural point of view, the mechanics of the weapon is a variant rifles AR-18, designed in the early 1960-ies in the USA, but is made largely of advanced materials, including high-strength polymers for the receiver.

In addition to the Bundeswehr H&K G36K in different variants is in service with more than 30 countries. The rifle is especially popular in the various police services, border guard and special forces. G36 was used in the fighting in Yugoslavia, Afghanistan, Iraq and South Ossetia.
