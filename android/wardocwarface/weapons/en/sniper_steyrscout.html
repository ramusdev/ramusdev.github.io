Historical note

Steyr Scout sniper rifle developed and manufactured by the Austrian company Steyr Mannlicher. Belongs to a special class of universal rifles "scout", designed for hunting, and for self-defense and combat.

In the classical configuration is easily recognizable by the optical scope made far ahead and located low on the trunk.

Rifle Steyr Scout is an exemplary representative of a class of rifles type "scout". This type of rifle was proposed and theoretically substantiated in the 1980-ies a well-known expert on firearms and techniques of shooting Jeff Cooper.

Before the Steyr Scout rifle type "scout" made only to order, in private. Sam Cooper was directly involved in the long development process, introducing into the design of the rifle a lot of original features that directly stemmed from his vision of universal rifle.



Thanks to these features, on the idea of Cooper Steyr Scout rifle can be used in a wide range of firing situations, promotes quick intuitive shooting offhand, suitable for long marches and unpredictable situations in long-term autonomy, extends the tactical Arsenal of the arrow.

According to the concept of Jeff Cooper, Steyr Scout should have accuracy not less than 2 MOA at a distance of 200 m and to guarantee the defeat of the growth targets at ranges up to 450 m without using the optical sight.

The basic version uses a Steyr Scout rifle cartridge 7.62×51 mm NATO, or cartridge 7mm-08 Remington. Also available rifle in .376 Steyr caliber (modified for a more powerful cartridge, dubbed Dragoon Scout) and 5.56х45 NATO (aka .223, less powerful version, dubbed the Cub Scout).

Technically is a rifle patented Staurowsky scheme a longitudinally sliding rotary shutter SBS, with detachable 10-round magazine. Due to the low weight and powerful cartridge has a fairly sharp and strong returns.
