Historical note

Tavor CTAR-21 - developed in 1990-ies the Israeli company Israel Military Industries and is part of a whole family of weapons under the designation "Tavor".
The development of IMI was first presented to the public in the mid 1990-ies under the designation "M-203". Later, to avoid confusion with the same grenade launcher, the machine was renamed the AAR (eng. Advanced Assault Rifle — the progressive assault rifle).



After some time the weapon was given the final name of the Tavor TAR 21. Unlike some other samples of this new machine was not a remake or modernization of other weapons.

Military trials TAR-21 took place in late 1990s-early 2000-ies. Was revealed some shortcomings of the machine: insecurity, difficulties in the change store in a lying position, too fat pistol grip, bad sight. In 2004, a modified version of TAR-21 entered service some parts of power structures of Israel.

In addition to the basic model assault rifle Tavor TAR 21 at its base were created Tavor CTAR 21 carbine, compact automatic Tavor MTAR 21, as well as a sniper rifle Tavor STAR 21. The name of The machine comes from mount Tabor in Northern Israel.

Submachine gun "CTAR-21" made on a "bullpup". "Linear" scheme (the butt is on the same axis with the barrel) which gives the weapon a high accuracy.
