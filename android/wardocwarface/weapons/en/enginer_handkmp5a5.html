Historical note

Submachine gun MP5 was first introduced by the firm Heckler & Koch in 1966, under the name HK54. This name was chosen according to the old numbering system models of the firm: the number "5" defined the weapon as a submachine gun, and the number "4" was determined that the gun was created under the cartridge 9×19 mm Parabellum.

The modern name he received after mid-1966, the Federal government took it on Board the police and border guard as the Maschinenpistole 5, or MP5 abbreviated.



Through the use of submachine gun of the German counter-terrorist squad GSG 9, which was part of the border service, special units similar to other Western countries got the opportunity to get acquainted with its characteristics.

Automatic MP5 operates on the principle of semi retention of the gate, firing from a closed bolt. The trigger mechanism is hammer type can fire in automatic and single mode. The fuse is also the translator of modes of fire.
