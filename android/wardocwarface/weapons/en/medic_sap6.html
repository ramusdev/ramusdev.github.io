The SAP6 in Warface is a pump-action shotgun that uses magazines to speed up reloading. But it's not just the quick reload that makes this weapon different. The SAP6 has a spring-loaded handguard, which makes reloading easier.

Combat Effectiveness

Good rate of fire and high damage make the SAP6 one of the best pump-action shotguns in the game. Its main competitors are the MAG-7 and Fabarm STF 12 Compact, which are the closest in terms of firing rate, accuracy and magazine capacity.

Historical background

The SAP6 shotgun was developed by the Turkish company Dagger Arms in 2014. When creating it, the designers wanted to move away from the concept of classic pump-action guns, focusing on small size and convenience.



The use of box magazines made it possible to achieve the goal — the length of the SAP6 barrel is 290 mm, as well as to achieve a high reloading speed. The telescopic butt has several positions, which also makes the shotgun more convenient.

The SAP6 uses 12-gauge cartridges, the standard magazine is designed for 6 such cartridges. Manual reloading is carried out using a movable handguard, which does not have a limiter and its stroke is screwed in front of the muzzle.

On the upper part of the receiver, almost the entire length of the Picatinny rail is located, which allows you to adapt the SAP6 to certain combat conditions.
