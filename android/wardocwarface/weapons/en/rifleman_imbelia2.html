Historical note

IMBEL IA2 — a modern assault rifle developed and manufactured by Brazilian company Industria de Material Belico do Brasil (IMBEL). In 2008, by order of the Armed forces of Brazil to replace in service with rifles MD-97 5.56 mm gun factory began the development of a new assault rifle chambered for NATO 5,56×45 mm.

The first prototypes of IA2 was shown in 2010, and in October 2013 this model was officially adopted by the Brazilian army and put into production.

The basis was taken earlier assault rifle of the same manufacturer MD-97, which underwent a significant modernization. The new weapon changes were venting automation, improved ergonomics and methods of use and maintenance, and use of modern polymer materials.

Structurally, the IMBEL IA2 is an automatic weapon that uses the vapor-automatic located above the barrel, gas piston with short stroke and gas regulator.

Receiver is made of aluminum alloy and is pivotally connected with a box trigger. A hinge is located between the magazine and the trigger guard. Box of the trigger performed by a single module with a pistol grip. When disassembling the box USM with pistol grip "fold" down and forward, opening access to the trigger and bolt group.



The trigger mechanism in a variant of an assault rifle permits firing single shots and bursts, and the carbine — only single. Translator-fuse located on the left side above the pistol grip.

Arm cocking bolt on the left side and not moving when shooting. Ejection of spent cartridges is on the right side of the weapon. Food arms ammunition is from detachable box magazine 30 rounds.

As a staff sighting devices used diopter open sights with the rear sight installations, the rocker 150 and 250 meters. In addition, for the optical or night sight on the receiver located on top of the universal guide type Picatinny rail.
