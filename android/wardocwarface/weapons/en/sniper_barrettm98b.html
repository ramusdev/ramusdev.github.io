Historical note

Barrett M98B — sniper rifle developed and manufactured by the American company "Barrett Firearms Manufacturing". Work on the development of sniper rifles was launched in 2000. In 2009, the sniper rifle went into production.

Barrett M98B works by manually reloading with a longitudinally-sliding rotating bolt. The grip shutter is directly behind the breech, which unloads the receiver, allowing the use of lightweight aluminum alloy for its manufacture.



The receiver is assembled from two parts (upper and lower) connected by a hinge in the front. USM Barrett 98B is a separate module that can be easily removed from the weapon by partial disassembly, allowing you to regulate downhill on the main parameters.

The barrel features a special longitudinal valleys to facilitate and improve cooling, and is also equipped with a muzzle brake. Straight shape of the rifle helps to reduce the negative effect of the impact. Pistol grip has an ergonomic shape.

The stock has adjustable butt pad and the stop under the cheek. At the bottom of the butt is a built-in adjustable retractable support monopod. Also on the rifle are mounted folding adjustable bipod.

Open sights rifle has not. On top of the receiver located universal guide type Picatinny, which are mounted optical or night sights. Guides of the same type located on the sides of the receiver in the front.

Rifle ammunition is fed from detachable box magazine 10 rounds. The rifle uses cartridges .338 Lapua Magnum caliber 8,6×70 mm.
