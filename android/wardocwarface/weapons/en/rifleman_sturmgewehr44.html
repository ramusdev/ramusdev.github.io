Historical background

The Sturmgewehr 44 is a German submachine gun developed in 1944 by Mauser Werke.

From submachine guns (PPSh), which were massively used in the Second World War, it was distinguished by the ability to conduct aimed fire at a much longer range, primarily due to the use of the so-called intermediate cartridge, more powerful and with better ballistics than the pistol cartridges used in submachine guns.



Among the machines of the modern type, it was the first development that was mass-produced. The total production of Sturmgewehr 44 for 1943-1945 was more than 400 thousand units, and in the post-war period its production was not resumed.

However, the Sturmgewehr 44 submachine guns were used in limited numbers in the early post-war period in the GDR and Czechoslovakia, and in Yugoslavia they remained in service with the airborne forces until the 1970s.
