Historical background

DP-27 (Degtyareva Infantry model 1927) became the first domestic light machine gun of mass production.

Its first samples were made at the Kovrov Factory on November 12, 1927, then a batch of 100 machine guns went to military tests, according to the results of which on December 21, 1927, the weapon was adopted by the Red Army.



The barrel of the machine gun had 6 rifling holes and was located in the casing, which provided protection for the shooter from burns during firing. The stock was made of wood, it was located in the oilcan and spare parts for the care of weapons.

Cartridges of the 7.62 x 54 mm caliber were placed in separate places in the disc magazine and did not cling to the neighboring ones with the edge, as happened in the carob magazines.

A special design with a front sight informed the fighter about how many rounds were left in the disk. If necessary, the store could be disassembled and cleaned of dirt. One of the main advantages of the machine gun is its reliability in difficult operating conditions.
