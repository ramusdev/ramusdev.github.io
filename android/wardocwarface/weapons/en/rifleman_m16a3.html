Historical note

M16A3 is an American assault rifle developed in 1992 on the basis of the AR-15 rifle. The weapon is manufactured by "FN Manufacturing Inc".

The beginning of the work on small-caliber weapons chambered for intermediate power between pistol and full size rifle in the USA was initiated as one of the results of the project "ALCLAD".

In its course were analyzed more than three million reports of deaths and injuries among soldiers on the battlefield during the period from the First world in the Korean war.

One result of this research was the conclusion about necessity of creation for the U.S. Army light weapons with a small recoil momentum, the leading auto-fire special lightweight bullets of small calibre, effective not more than 400-500 meters to compensate for high rate of fire and higher probability of hitting the target when you fire bursts less the weight of a single bullet.



In 1957, the army command United States launched a competition to create a light military rifle with a caliber of about 5.5—5.6 mm.

Tactical and technical requirements included, in addition to caliber, the possibility of single and automatic fire, 20-cartridge store, mass with not more than 3 kg and the ability to punch through a standard army helmet with 500 m.
