Historical background

The M1928 automatic submachine gun was developed in 1918 by John Thompson and belongs to a relatively small family of caliber submachine guns .45 ACP. This weapon was widely used first during the "Prohibition", and after-during the Second World War.

The Thompson submachine gun (or Tommy gun) has long been in service with the military, police, and FBI agents. Now this gun is often found in gangster movies.

Despite the complexity of the design and expensive production, the weapon was distinguished by reliability and high quality of manufacture. The M1928 model is a modification of the first production model M1921. It has two fire modes, a finned barrel equipped with a muzzle compensator, and a reduced rate of fire.

The weapon was produced with both a vertical front handle and a conventional wooden handguard.
