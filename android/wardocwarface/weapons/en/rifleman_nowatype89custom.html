An improved version of the Howa Type 89 rifle, which is in service with the Self-Defense Forces, the special Assault team and the Japanese security team. It has never been exported outside Japan due to its strict anti-export policy.

Known by the nickname"Buddy". The pride of Japanese gunsmiths.

Combat Effectiveness

The closest competitors of the Howa Type 89 Custom in terms of combat characteristics are the AK-12 and the CZ 805 BREN A2. Among the main advantages of this weapon — damage, range and slightly increased damage multiplier on the legs.



The gun is effective at any distance, is able to hit the enemy with a single hit to the head, and also has an easily controlled recoil and modification capabilities. The assault rifle comes with three unique modules.

Historical background

The machine developed in 1989 by the Japanese company Howa Machinery Company Ltd. It is available in three versions-basic, with a folding metal butt, as well as in a modification with a two-way safety-translator and an additional shooting mode with a cut-off of 3 shots.

The Howa Type 89 is a landmark gun for Japan, which is still in use today. The model is designed on the basis of the American AR-18 platform, which, in comparison with the AR-15, has a more advanced device with a gas piston that has a short working stroke.

A special feature of the Howa Type 89 gas exhaust system is the structure of the gas piston, which consists of two parts. This design provides a two-stage power transfer, which contributes to a smoother operation.

The trigger mechanism allows for single and automatic fire. When installing a separate module, it is possible to shoot cut-offs of 3 shots. The body of the model is made of steel, the forearm is made of aluminum, and the butt and handle are made of plastic.
