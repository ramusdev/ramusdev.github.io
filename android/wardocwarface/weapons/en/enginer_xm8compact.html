Historical note

XM8 — a complex of small arms, developed jointly by the American arms company ATK (Alliant Techsystems) and the German company Heckler & Koch-based rifles H&K G36.
HM8 developed primarily to replace the M16 rifles and M4A1 with less weight and greater reliability. When using new ammunition with a lightweight composite plastic casing the new machine provided saving weight by 20% compared to the M16.


Start of production of this weapon was scheduled for about 2006. In 2004, the machines have undergone extensive testing in the U.S. armed forces, earning positive reviews. Despite this, in October 2005, the project was officially closed.
HM8 based on automation with a short stroke gas piston, located above the barrel, and locking by a rotating bolt with 7 lugs. The receiver and forend made of durable plastic.
The butt is adjustable along the length of the telescoping five-position, easily replaceable, also made of plastic. Above the pistol grip is a two-way fuse-translator fire mode, which allows you to fire single shots and continuous bursts. The barrel has a high survivability (over 20,000 shots).
