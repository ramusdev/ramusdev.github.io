Historical note

M14 Crazy Horse — American marczynska rifle designed by special operations forces of the U.S. Navy based on the M14 rifle in 2001. Adopted by the US Navy in 2004 and operated to this day.

The main differences from the M14: new Lodge (development company Smith Enterprises), telescoping buttstock, pistol grip, set the guide type Picatinny rail and some "commercial" components, for example, a new precision barrel and gas cylinder.



The new rifle was of little use to new at the time of war, that was quickly revealed fighting the United States in Southeast Asia, especially in Vietnam. The M14 rifle was too long for fighting in the jungle, a large mass of each cartridge reduced carry ammunition to unacceptably small fire bursts from the rifle, any sighting was only possible with a bipod or rest, but only for a small range.

In General, most rifles were issued to soldiers with the removed interpreter modes of fire, as in most cases, shooting bursts was nothing more than a waste of ammunition. The translator could be set back on the rifle in the field.

Precisely because of the above, the US Army had to adopt a new model, under -, malinowsky intermediate cartridge 5.56 mm Colt/Armalite AR-15 / M16. However, the M14 rifles still are in service with the US Navy, more Special Operations Forces of the Navy.
