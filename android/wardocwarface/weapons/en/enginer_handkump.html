Historical note

Heckler & Koch Universale Maschinenpistole (UMP) - "universal submachine gun", which was created by the German company Heckler und Koch in 1996. The weapon was developed as a Supplement to rather expensive MP5 - pistol-machine gun of the same company.

H&K UMP was positioned as a more modern and cheaper option. The gun was designed primarily for the American market, so initially it was used popular in the USA ammunition .45АСР and .40SW. He was subsequently released under the 9 mm Parabellum cartridge.

From a technical point of view, H&K UMP is a weapon, built under the scheme with a free gate. Fires from closed bolt, trigger trigger, with two bilateral switches fire mode.
