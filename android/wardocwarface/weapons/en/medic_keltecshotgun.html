Kel-Tec KSG-pump-action shotgun 12-gauge, made according to the scheme of bull-pop. The main feature of the weapon is the use of two tubular magazines, which are switched manually. The capacity of each magazine is 7 rounds.

Acquisition

Kel-Tec Shotgun can be purchased in the game store ("Weapons») both for permanent loans and for long-term warbucks (7 or 30 days).

There is also a chance to get this weapon for a while (1-7 days) as a gift for daily entry.

Combat Effectiveness

The weapon has good characteristics. Excellent damage, good range and accuracy of the shotgun will allow you to shoot comfortably at both short and medium distances. Great chance to kill with the first shot. If the opponent survives (which is unlikely), the player has a chance to finish him off with a second shot.

For ammunition, the shotgun uses a tubular magazine with the ability to replenish the magazine with one cartridge. It is equally suitable for all game modes and does not require special handling skills.

Weapons are purchased with credits, which gives players the opportunity to buy this shotgun at any rank. If the weapon is purchased on time, it frees the owner from having to repair the shotgun. If you buy a weapon permanently after using it, you will need to repair it.

A roomy magazine is enough to easily defeat a whole squad of the enemy.

Historical background

The Kel-Tec Shotgun is a smoothbore rifle developed in 2011 by the American company "Kel-tec CNC industries" and produced by it to this day. For the first time this shotgun was presented to the public in January 2011 at the gun show "SHOT Show" in Las Vegas. This is the first shotgun produced by Kel-tec CNC.

The weapon has a rather unusual design for its type, small dimensions and solid ammunition. First of all, the shotgun is positioned as a self-defense weapon for the civilian population. In addition, it is of interest to law enforcement agencies and security units.

On the technical side, the Kel-Tec Shotgun is not an automatic rifle, built according to the "bull-pop"scheme. The shotgun uses a manual reload with a longitudinally sliding handguard. The handguard is made of high-strength plastic and is connected to the shutter by means of two metal rods.

Locking of the barrel bore is carried out by a swinging combat larva, fixed in the upper part of the bolt and coupling it with the barrel shank.

In the upper part of the barrel there is a Picatinny rail guide bar, on which various types of open or collimator sights can be installed. An additional guide of the same type is located on the forearm and allows you to put a tactical flashlight, laser sight or front handle.

Power is provided from two tubular magazines located parallel to each other under the barrel, each of which holds up to 7 rounds of 12 caliber. The magazines are loaded and the cartridges are ejected from the same window at the bottom of the receiver, behind the pistol grip.
