Historical note

PP-2000 — Russian 9mm submachine gun, developed by a team of designers Tula instrument design Bureau under the leadership of P. V. gryazeva and A. G. shipunova.

Work on the creation of a new submachine gun was developed in the framework of the ROC "Bakinets". The patent for the design was obtained in 2001, but the General public, he was first introduced in 2004 — in the summer at the Paris exhibition "Eurosatory 2004" and fall at the Moscow "INTERPOLITEX-2004".



PP-2000 is positioned as a weapon for law enforcement units operating in urban environments, as well as a personal defense weapon for separate categories of servicemen.

The result of pilot operation of pre-production samples on the basis of Department of private security at the municipal Department of internal Affairs of the Moscow region in the basic design of the PP-2000 "was made some improvements to more efficiently and safely use the gun in an urban setting".

Then in 2006 began mass production of PP-2000 for the interior Ministry.

The gun is based on automation with a free gate, fires from a closed bolt. Trigger mechanism — a hammer type. Case PP-2000 is made of durable plastic. The bore is chrome-plated. The pistol grip is traditional for a compact submachine guns is also the neckline for the store.

Massive trigger guard can be used as handle fire control, and also allows you to shoot without removing thick gloves. A feature of this gun is the ability to use a backup store as a shoulder rest.

Fuse-translator fire mode is located only on the left side of the weapon, but the cocking lever and the shutter button of the magazine catch can be repositioned on any side.

Serial PP-2000 since 2006 are equipped with a removable side-folding metal shoulder rest, and at the arms exhibition "DEFEXPO" (in February 2010) was presented with a more convenient clumsy plastic butt skeletal structure.
