Historical note

American compact sniper rifle McMillan CS5 (Concealable Subsonic — "silent subsonic"). Developed by McMillan in 2005. Equipped with quick-detachable silencer. Is in service with special forces.

Gail McMillan (Gale McMillan), founder of McMillan began to manufacture weapons for its own use in 1973. Today, the family company McMillan is well known throughout the world as a manufacturer of superior small arms.



One of the last examples is the sniper rifle McMillan CS5™. It is a low noise subsonic/supersonic sniper system designed for concealed and disguised use in urban criteria.

Rotary rifle with a longitudinally sliding gate CS5 has been specifically designed as a compact, low-noise, high-precision weapons. The shortened and the standard configuration is intended for use as paramilitary and law enforcement units.

With the removal of the buttstock and a silencer length CS5 is only 580 mm, which allows it to be placed in a backpack or other not attract attention to the case.

The use of subsonic ammunition 0.308 Winchester CS5 allows to reach the accuracy of 0.2 mrad (0.75 of a minute of a degree), and in urban areas it can be even better. The same accuracy could be achieved with a typical 7.62 mm NATO ammunition.

Thus, the firearm can be used with subsonic ammunition, and in the role of a traditional sniper rifle.
