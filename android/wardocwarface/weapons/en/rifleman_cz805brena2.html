A shortened model of the Czech machine gun CZ 805 with a cartridge of 5.56x45 mm. The weapon is equipped with a folding plastic butt and removable sighting devices.

Combat Effectiveness

The CZ 805 BREN A2 is an assault rifle with impressive damage and range, as well as soft recoil. Its balanced characteristics make it an excellent means of eliminating the enemy at almost any distance.

The main competitors of the new "Czech" will be the F90 MBR and AK-12, from which the rifle has a higher rate of fire and damage.

The machine has a unique handle, and also deals increased damage to the limbs.

Historical background

The CZ 805 is a promising Czech weapon system, introduced in 2009 and intended to replace the CZ SA Vz.58. In 2010, it was announced that this system won the competition over the Belgian FN SCAR and signed a contract for the supply of several thousand of these machines.

The CZ 805 automatic system is traditional: the removal of powder gases from the barrel bore and the locking of the barrel by turning the bolt.

The receiver is made of aluminum alloy, the USM box with a pistol grip and the magazine shaft are made of polymers. The two-way fuse-translator of firing modes has 4 positions corresponding to the fuse, single, bursts with a cut-off of 2 shots and continuous bursts.

The cocking handle can be moved to the left side for easy use by left-handers. Standard magazines are made of transparent plastic, so that the shooter can control the consumption of ammunition, STANAG-compatible magazines can be used after changing the magazine shaft.

The removable plastic stock is adjustable in length and folds to the right. As additional accessories, a 40-mm CZ G 805 grenade launcher or a bayonet-knife, a silencer, an additional front handle combined with bipods, a tactical flashlight, and so on can be installed.
