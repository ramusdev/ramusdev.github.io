A light assault rifle developed by the Australian company Thales. Allows the shooter to swim with a rifle and open fire immediately after leaving the water, so great for amphibious troops who land in the seas.

Combat Effectiveness

The F90 MBR in Warface is an assault rifle with impressive damage and range. Balanced characteristics make it a universal means of eliminating the enemy at almost any distance.

The main competitor of the F90 MBR is the AK-12, from which the rifle differs in a higher rate of fire and recoil force (it can be compensated by installing a unique handle).

The machine has a unique handle, and also deals increased damage to the limbs.

Historical background

The F90 MBR was presented at the international defense technology exhibition DSEi 2017 by the Australian division of Thales, a major manufacturer of military equipment. The platform for the novelty was the F90 rifle, which was in service with the Australian Defense Forces — and it, in turn, is a deep modernization of the F88.

The new rifle has a gas-discharge automatic and a trigger mechanism of the original design, but differs in the presence of a bolt delay, which was absent from the previous rifles of the AUG family. Caliber F90MBR — 5.56×45 mm NATO.

Thanks to the "bull-pop" layout with a relatively short body, the F90 MBR has a long barrel that provides optimal muzzle velocity, excellent range and accuracy.

And if you add to the small size and the use of composite materials in the design, then we get one of the lightest assault rifles on the market: its weight is 3.25 kg.

The convenience and versatility of the rifle add the ability to quickly change barrels (MBR in the name means "modular bullpup rifle") and the presence of two-way controls. The F90MBR uses standard NATO slats and magazines.
