Historical note

PM-84 Glauberyt (Polish. yeah maszynowy wz.84) is a Polish 9 mm submachine gun developed in the early 1980-ies of the designers Ryszard Chelmicki, Janusz Hancewicze and Stanislav BRICs for the Polish army and police to replace the not quite successful submachine gun PM-63 RAK.

Adopted in 1984. There were separate categories of the military (paratroopers, the crews of armored vehicles), police, border guards, railway, and postal security.



From the first glance it is clear that PM-84 Glauberyt is more than just the enhanced version of the PM-63 with some technological improvements, to which was added the design points, borrowed from UZI.

Available PM-84 Glauberyt factory "Slayed Metalowe" which is a solely state-owned weapons factory producing at the time, the Wz.63. Project work in PM-84 Glauberyt was conducted in the centre for development and research of Radom under the leadership of Chief designer Richard Helmick.

PM-84 looks like a slightly elongated PM-63. The principle of operation of automation remains the same — the free-bolt recoil, which before firing is in the front position and a back cartridge in the chamber.

Therefore, PM-84 Glauberyt a spring-loaded striker and a trigger located inside the receiver. Used cartridge remained the same — 9x18 mm Makarov, although the weapon can easily be adapted for firing a cartridge 9x19mm Parabellum (PM-84P).

The stores remained unchanged from the PM-63 and inserted into the pistol grip, which is made checki single piece of black plastic.

The details of the weapon made mostly by stamping from sheet metal and heat resistant plastic. In appearance the PM-84 Glauberyt looks like a cross between a PM-63 and normal UZI. On the muzzle of the barrel can be installed muffler.

In the pistol grip, PM-84 Glauberyt is the mechanism of slowing the rate of fire, similarly as in the Czech Skorpion vz 61. It has a spring loaded inertia retarder, which moves up and down, perpendicular to the axis of the bore. Despite its complexity, this mechanism is apparently quite effective when rate of fire is only 600 rounds per minute.

It is remarkable that the PM-84 Glauberyt there are two ways to switch firing modes. Immediately behind the pistol grip on the housing of the receiver is the interpreter-fuse. Its top position corresponds to the position of "protection".

At this position the shutter and the trigger is blocked. The rotation of the interpreter in the middle position will correspond to the semi-automatic mode. In the lower position of the gun will be automatic gunfire. br>
In addition, in the same way as on the PM-63 with a slight press of the trigger you can fire single shots, it shooting will be automatic.
