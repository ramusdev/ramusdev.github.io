Historical note

WA 2000 (Walther Automat 2000) — sniper rifle developed by the German arms concern "Carl Walther Waffenfabrik" in the early 1980s for police and counterterrorism units.

According to the company Walther released was only 176 rifles of this type. Unlike many other rifles of the same period, the WA 2000 was originally designed as a specialized weapon, and had a number of interesting features.

Structurally, the WA 2000 is a semi-automatic weapon, built on the vapor circuit with a locking rotating bolt. Gas piston with short stroke located under the barrel.

The rifle is made in bullpup configuration, thus able to achieve sufficient compactness of all weapons with a fairly long barrel. The design of put aluminum frame of the two longitudinal tire located over and under the barrel and a special United front screed and the rear receiver of the weapon.

Frame provides support for the elements of the Lodge of the weapon, an optical sight and bipod, protecting the loads from a cantilever posted trunk. Bed adjustable for arrow length of the butt and the location of the stop for the cheeks.

The trigger mechanism is adjustable by the force of the descent and the go trigger. The protection against accidental shots is provided by a non-automatic fuse, the engine of which is located on the right side of the receiver above the trigger. In addition to the fuse on the right side of the receiver are: the window to eject spent shell casings, cocking lever and the shutter key slide stop.

The rifle was produced from 1982 until November 1988. Production was discontinued due to excessive cost of the product compared to other sniper rifles. The latest instances were implemented in 1988 at a price of 9 to 12.5 thousand dollars.

According to some estimates, currently the cost of one rifle, the WA 2000 is around 40-50 thousand dollars, according to some information — up to 75 thousand dollars. Experts say the best ergonomics of the weapon, typical of many German, Austrian and Swiss developments.
