Historical note

PP-19 'Bizon' submachine gun which was developed by V. M. Kalashnikov (son of the designer, Mikhail Kalashnikov) and Alexei Dragunov (son of E. F. Dragunov) at the Izhevsk machine-building plant in 1993 for the armament of the MIA of Russia.

PP-19 is built with extensive use of components and parts from a Kalashnikov AK-74, in particular, the use of a shortened receiver of the AK-74 trigger mechanism and pistol grip.



The original store high capacity is designed, obviously, without the influence of American submachine guns Calico, but PP-19 store is more convenient and performs in parallel the role of the forearm.

In General, the 'bison' is quite comfortable to hold and aim, largely due to the full side folding butt and a chunky easy grip forend store.

Automatic PP-19 is based on the free slide recoil, the trigger mechanism of hammer type, with fuse borrowed from AK-74. Fires from closed bolt, which improves the accuracy of firing single shots.
