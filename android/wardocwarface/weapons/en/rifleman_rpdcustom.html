Historical background

The Degtyarev light machine gun (RPD) appeared back in 1944 and served as an infantry support weapon until the mid-sixties. The power is supplied from a 100-round cartridge belt placed in a round metal box.



Thanks to the carrying belt, the machine gun allows you to shoot from the hip. After some time, the design appeared bipods and the ability to install on a tripod machine. All this makes the machine gun suitable for any situation.

On the basis of this weapon, a number of modifications were created, including for the civilian market. In accordance with the latest trends, the weapon received a new "body kit" in the form of numerous Picatinny slats, modern sighting devices, telescopic butts, ergonomic handles and other modules.

It is safe to say that the RPD is still a modern weapon that meets all the necessary requirements for combat.
