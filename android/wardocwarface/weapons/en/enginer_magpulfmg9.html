Historical note

The idea of creating a folding submachine gun continued in 2008, when at Magpul Industries SHOTShow 2008 presented the prototype of the Magpul FMG-9.

The new product replicates the look of the Ares FMG, but made with the latest technology. The case is made of lightweight and high-strength polymeric materials. Folded Magpul FMG-9 looks quite harmless, resembling a small case with a handle. The manufacturer expected that the development will be interested in US Secret service and other special forces.



The name FMG is an acronym for "Folding Machine Gun" (folding submachine gun).

In order not to reinvent the wheel, engineers from Magpul Industries took as a basis the well-proven Glock 18 pistol chambered 9 mm Parabellum (9×19 mm NATO). Stores uses standard 30-33 rounds from a Glock 18.
