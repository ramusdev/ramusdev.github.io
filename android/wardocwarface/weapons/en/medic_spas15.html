Historical note

SPAS-15 (Special Purpose Automatic Shotgun-15) — a combat shotgun developed by the Italian company "Luigi Franchi S. p.A.". Established in 1984 in accordance with the new requirements of the Ministry of interior of Italy.

SPAS-15 was designed based on shotgun SPAS-12. The shotgun was originally designed for arming the police and army, on the civilian market it is targeted not been. In 1986, the shotgun came into production. But the weapon did not stick, the model was unsuccessful, so was discontinued in 2005.

Shotgun runs as a shotgun. After filling the store with bullets, should drastically distort the fore-end back and forth. During the backward motion will be cocked, and when moving forward to tosylates new cartridge from the store. That is all done by hand, by human muscle power, aimed at moving the forearm.

Appearance SPAS–15 is obviously far removed from its predecessor in design, acquiring a militarized design, this rifle is made in the style of "Military".

Receiver rifles made of aluminum alloy, made possible for safe handling only because the locking of the cartridge chamber by turning the bolt and cooperates with the barrel and not to the receiver. Aluminum alloy as the material of the receiver significantly reduces the overall weight of the weapon.

On the SPAS-15 is the rear sight located on the carrying handle. Front sight mounted on the far end of the venting tube. That is, the sighting line is quite short and high, which justifies itself in combat arms, but not suitable for hunting if you are shooting with buckshot. Front sight and rear sight unregulated.

In addition to the open sight, it's possible to install additional sighting devices, such as collimator and optical sights, laser designators and night vision sights, as well as the installation of lights.

SPAS-15 is armed 6-charger boxed plastic store single-row arrangement of cartridges, using bullets of the 12th caliber with the sleeve 70 mm.

The shotgun is equipped with a butt pipe skeleton type aluminium alloy, folding to the left side and a carrying handle which is located above the receiver.

Shotgun was adopted in many countries as weapons for law enforcement, and for use by army special forces. Subsequently, SPAS-15 was used as a hunting weapon and as a weapon for recreational shooting, then went to the civilian market.

In Russia the modification of this shotgun was approved for sale to citizens and certified as "smoothbore rifle", although in reality it is not.
