Historical note

Shotgun SPAS-12 was developed in the 70-ies of XX century by the Italian company Luigi Franchi. For it was taken as the basis of a self-loading hunting gun Luigi Franchi PG 80. The production of the SPAS-12 began in 1979 and continues until now.

The shotgun was initially positioned as a universal military weapons for police and military. Accurate opinion on the decoding of abbreviations SPAS there - it reads as either Sporting Purpose Automatic Shotgun (automatic shotgun sports), or as Special Purpose Automatic Shotgun (automatic shotgun special purpose).

SPAS-12 is widespread in police and special forces and also used civilians for self-defense and participation in sports competitions. The gun is highly reliable, but has a rather large weight and complex structure. The shotgun usually comes with a metal folding stock and plastic pistol grip.

Italian shotgun SPAS-12 can operate in two modes. The first mode used in "Warface" – manually reload with a movable forearm that is connected to the gas piston, the so-called "pump system" (Pump Action). The second mode of weapon – semi-automatic.
