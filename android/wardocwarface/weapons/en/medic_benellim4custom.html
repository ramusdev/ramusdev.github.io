Historical background

The M4 is Benelli's first self-loading rifle with gas-discharge automation. Its simplicity, durability and originality quickly won the public — and the weapon entered the service of soldiers. The system consists of two main parts: a gas chamber and a short-stroke gas piston.



The patented gas exhaust system guarantees high accuracy of fire, as well as the reliability and durability of the weapon. This made the M4 one of the best combat shotguns of the US Armed Forces.
