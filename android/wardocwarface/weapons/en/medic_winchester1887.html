Historical background

The Winchester Model 1887 is a lever-action rifle designed by the famous American gunsmith John Browning in 1887 and produced by the Winchester Repeating Arms Company from 1887 to 1920.

Video by Elez



The M1887 was the first truly successful multi-shotgun. Initially, John Browning suggested the pump mechanism as more suitable for a multi-shot gun, but Winchester insisted on retaining the "lever", more recognizable brand.

However, Winchester later introduced the Browning pump-action shotgun: the Model 1893 (an early version of the Model 1897), after the introduction of smokeless powder.
