Historical background

The M4 carbine is a development of the company "Colt's Manufacturing Company LLC", whose engineers in 1984 decided to create a shortened version of the M16A2. The weapon went into mass production and was used by the US Army in 1994 with the general designation "M4 Carbine".

The outstanding characteristics of the new carbine were appreciated, which allowed it to become the main individual weapon for all special forces. Now most of the M4 carbines in the semi-automatic (self-loading) civilian version are produced by the weapons company "Bushmaster Firearms International LLC".
