Historical note

The Daewoo K7 is a South Korean submachine gun developed by a division of South Korean Corporation "Daewoo Precision Industries" for the weapons of the special forces.

The design of the Daewoo K7 is based on the submachine gun Daewoo K1. The upper part of the receiver is taken from the Daewoo K2 rifle and modified. The Daewoo K7 is used in the cartridges 9×19 mm Parabellum subsonic bullets. PP uses automation with a free gate, not the exhaust gases as the Daewoo K1.

Fires from closed bolt, trigger is used the trigger mechanism from the rifle Daewoo K2 together to the receiver. The gun can fire single, bursts with a cutoff of three rounds and automatically.

But automatic fire can damage the baffles in the muffler. From this borrowed rifle sights and pistol grip. Sliding retractable stock is borrowed from a Daewoo K1.

The gun has an integrated silencer shot sound, located around the barrel and partly hidden by the fore end. The use of the gun without silencer is not assumed.

It is designed for arming special operations forces of the army and police, as a cheaper alternative to a submachine gun H&K MP5SD German production.
