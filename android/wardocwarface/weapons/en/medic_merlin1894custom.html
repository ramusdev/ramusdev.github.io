Historical background

The Marlin 1894 hunting rifle was developed by the American company Marlin Firearms Co. back in 1894. At that time, lever-action reloading weapons were gaining popularity, and American hunters were well-received with the new rifle with the "Henry bracket".



The simple design provided fast reloading and high reliability in the field. Despite the external similarity with the "Winchesters" of that time, the Marlin 1894 had a number of differences in the design of the receiver, the bolt assembly and the locking assembly.

The original Marlin 1894 weighed 2.95 kg with a length of 952 mm. She used caliber cartridges .44 Magnum and .44 Special, but today there are a large number of modifications for a variety of ammunition.

Gunsmiths remake the classics of the Wild West beyond recognition, using modern technologies-Picattini bars, collimator sights, silent shooting devices, and so on.
