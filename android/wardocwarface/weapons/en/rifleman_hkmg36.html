Historical note

After the unification in 1990 of East and West Germany needed to develop a new, not too expensive, but it's a good weapons system for the new country. In this regard, in 1992, the military leadership of Germany raised the question of the development of assault rifles and light machine guns unified with her. Soon the Heckler & Koch had submitted a fairly cheap family of weapons – MG50 machine gun and assault rifle НК50.

In 1996, they were accepted by the Bundeswehr under the designations H&K MG36 H&K G36, respectively, and began mass production of this complex of small arms. To speed up development was used to layout the experimental rifle НК36, which the firm created back in the late 1970's, USM from G3, and a modified scheme of automation from the American M16 assault rifle.

One of the features of the assault rifle H&K G36 is the ability to quickly rework in installation MG36 heavy barrel and double-disc ("double drum") store C-MAG for 100 rounds. In both cases, the barrel length is the same. For parts of special purpose created the rifle G36K barrel length which is 318 millimeters.

Many details of this family of weapons are not strict tolerances in production, which gives the opportunity to use a rifle in the heavy pollution and large dust. Also to reduce the cost and simplify the production in the construction of weapons (handguard, a box of percussion trigger mechanism, shops, butt) are widely used polymer materials of high impact glass-filled polyamide

Metal parts of the rifle are covered with special insulating coating. It significantly reduced heat radiation weapons when firing, and, therefore, reducing the telltale signs, complicating thereby the enemy notch shooters at night imaging devices.
