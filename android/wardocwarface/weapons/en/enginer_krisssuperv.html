Historical note

Kriss Super V submachine gun produced by the TDI (Transformational Defense Industries). The first prototype submachine gun Kriss Vector appeared in 2004, mass production of submachine guns Kriss Super V only began in 2009.

Kriss Super V uses a semi-automatic shutter, a guide movable recoil bolt group down at a large angle. That is why this weapon has a smooth, controlled impact and toss the barrel only affects the high rate of fire.

In addition, the unique design of this machine pistol provides excellent accuracy. All this makes the Kriss Super V is a worthy representative of a number of submachine guns.

At the moment there are two main versions of the machine gun "military version" Vector SMG, designed for law enforcement agencies, law enforcement and military short (140 mm) barrel and automatic fire, the civilian version of the Vector CRB/SO with a longer to 406 mm barrel

(Under American law, the civil long-barreled weapons should have a barrel length less than 16 inches, otherwise it is considered a handgun / pistol licence to get in some States is much harder), on top of which is mounted a simulator muffler for greater aesthetics, and the ability to fire only in semiautomatic mode.
