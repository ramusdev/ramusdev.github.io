VHS-2 Croatian machine gun, made in the layout of the bull-pop. It is equipped with a carrying handle, a length-adjustable buttstock, and spent shell casings ejection windows on both sides of the weapon.

Acquisition

Weapons can be purchased for a time with credits.

Combat Effectiveness

VHS-2 is a legendary rifle, the choice of the professional. From other guns, it differs, first of all, in the damage of 84 units, the rate of fire and pliable recoil.

The weapon has good characteristics. Excellent damage and range, and high accuracy will allow you to shoot comfortably both at close range and at medium and high distances.

Automation is based on the direct discharge of powder gases from the bore to the bolt group, locking is performed by turning the bolt.

The body is plastic, the ejection of the cartridges occurs only in the right side (a similar feature of some bull-pop machine guns causes difficulties for left-handed shooters), but the cocking handle of the bolt is located on top and can be bent in both directions for ease of use.

The machine has an integral carrying handle, in which the sighting devices are also located and on which, as on the forearm, it is possible to install Picatinny bars. A bayonet-knife can be attached to the barrel, and it is also possible to throw rifle grenades from it.

The use of this weapon is allowed in Warface tournaments.

Historical background

The VHS was designed to replace the Zastava M70 submachine gun with a weapon that meets NATO standards.



The first version with a patented gas recoil buffer of the bolt group, which had a significant external resemblance to the TAR-21, was presented in 2005, but the pre-production version, demonstrated at international exhibitions in 2008, used a conventional spring instead of a buffer, and looked more like a French FAMAS machine gun.

The military tests of the weapon were successful, and in 2009 Croatia purchased 1,000 submachine guns of this model.
