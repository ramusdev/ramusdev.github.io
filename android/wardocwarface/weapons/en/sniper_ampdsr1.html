Historical note

AMP DSR-1 — sniper rifle with a longitudinally-sliding rotating bolt, developed in Germany in 2000 the company "DSR-precision GmbH" and produced to this day.

DSR-1 was designed as a specialized sniper rifle for use in the operations of police and counterterrorism plan requiring maximum accuracy and providing a fairly simple (compared with military operations) operating conditions.

For shooting the DSR-1 used rifle cartridges caliber 7.62 mm NATO (.308 Win), .300 Win Mag and .338 Lapua Mag. Submission rounds when firing is made from detachable box magazine capacity of 5 rounds. Store single-row, extra magazine is located in a special slot in front of the trigger guard.



Sniper rifle DSR-1, built in layout "bullpup". Barrel cantilever posted with longitudinal dales to reduce weight and improve cooling, equipped with a massive muzzle brake. Quick change barrel, fixed into receiver by three screws.

Butterfly valve has six radial lugs at the front and is locked by turning right behind the breech. The stock is fully adjustable, with adjustable pillow under his cheek, back support and adjustable fore-aft position.

Folding bipod attached to the special guides on aluminum forearm and provide the necessary movement of the rifle in all three axes.
